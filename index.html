Smith Wigglesworth

A man of faith!

The Lifetime of Smith Wigglesworth:

  • 1859: Born on June 8 in Menston, West Yorkshire, England.
  • 1877: Started working as a plumber at age 18 and was a devout member of the Wesleyan Methodist Church.
  • 1882: Married to Mary Jane, who significantly influenced his spiritual journey.
  • 1907: Converted to Christianity after being inspired by a meeting held by a Methodist minister.
  • 1909: Began his ministry, focusing on faith healing and revivals after a profound experience of the Holy Spirit.
  • 1914: Became a prominent evangelist known for his faith healing, attracting large crowds in the UK.
  • 1919: Conducted a successful revival campaign in Australia, establishing an international reputation.
  • 1920: His healing meetings gained notoriety, and he reportedly raised several people from the dead, which drew even larger audiences.
  • 1925: Authored several books, including "Smith Wigglesworth on Faith," sharing his teachings on faith and healing.
  • 1930: He toured the United States, further expanding his influence and drawing considerable attention to his faith healing practices.
  • 1935: Established the "Wigglesworth Trust" to support his ministry and distribute his teachings globally.
  • 1947: Continued to preach and conduct healing services until his health began to decline.
  • 1948: Died on March 12 in Cardiff, Wales, leaving a lasting legacy in the Pentecostal movement.
  • 1950s: Posthumously, his teachings and healing methods became foundational in the development of the Charismatic and Pentecostal movements.
  • 1993: Recognized by the Assemblies of God as a significant figure in the history of Pentecostalism, with a lasting impact on faith healing practices.
